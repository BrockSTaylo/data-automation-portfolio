# Upwork Cover Letter Template
## Data Automation & Document Processing Jobs

Use this template for jobs involving PDFs, spreadsheets, data cleanup, or automation.
Customize the bracketed sections per job before sending.

---

## Template

```
Hi [Client Name or "there"],

I looked at your job and I think I can solve this cleanly.
[ONE SENTENCE: describe their specific problem back to them — show you actually read it.]

Here’s exactly how I’d approach it:

1. [Step 1 — be specific, e.g. "Parse your PDFs using Python’s pdfplumber library to extract the table data"]
2. [Step 2 — e.g. "Clean and normalize the output into a structured CSV/Sheet"]
3. [Step 3 — e.g. "Deliver with a short plain-English explanation so you can rerun it yourself"]

I’m a CS student with hands-on experience in Python, data structures, and document processing.
I don’t just use AI to generate output — I write and verify the actual code.

Fixed price for this: $[your number]. Turnaround: [X days].
Happy to answer any questions before you decide.

— Taylor
```

---

## How to use this

**Step 1:** Find a job on Upwork. Good search terms:
- `extract data from PDF`
- `clean Excel spreadsheet`
- `organize invoices`
- `Google Sheets automation`
- `Python data processing`

Filter: **Fixed Price**, **$10–$100**, **Posted this week**

**Step 2:** Paste the job description to Claude and say:
> "Help me write one sentence describing their problem back to them, and a 3-step approach I’d use to solve it."

**Step 3:** Drop Claude’s output into the template above and adjust as needed.

**Step 4:** Bid $20–50 for your first few jobs. The goal is reviews, not rate.

## Tips

- **Never send a generic proposal.** Always reference something specific in their job post.
- **Short is better.** 3–5 sentences beats a wall of text.
- **Include your GitHub link** in your profile bio, not in every proposal.
- **First 3 reviews matter most.** Consider pricing at cost just to get them.
